Mint2Metal Protocol

Bridging the gap between digital asset representation and physical commodity custody. A Reserve-First architecture ensuring 1:1 allocated backing at all times.

1. Executive Summary

Mint2Metal solves the fundamental disconnect between digital asset representation and physical commodity custody. Unlike existing "paper silver" derivatives or fractional-reserve tokens, Mint2Metal employs a strict Reserve-First architecture.

Digital Silver Tokens (DST) are minted strictly after physical settlement and custodial verification are confirmed on-chain. The protocol bridges Indian retail demand for physical redemption with global Web3 liquidity, ensuring 1:1 allocated backing at all times.

2. Problem Statement

Current silver investment mechanisms suffer from trust deficits and operational inefficiencies.

Counterparty Risk

Most silver investments are "unallocated," meaning investors are unsecured creditors of a bank rather than owners of specific metal. In a default, you are last in line.

Verification Latency

Competitor protocols often mint tokens upon fiat receipt (T+0), acquiring metal days later (T+2). This creates a dangerous window of temporary fractional reserves.

Jurisdictional Friction

Cross-border physical redemption is legally complex. Most "global redemption" promises are largely unenforceable for retail users due to regulatory conflicts.

3. The Solution: Deterministic Minting

Mint2Metal enforces a strict dependency chain: Physical Custody ‚Üí Oracle Verification ‚Üí Token Minting.

Reserve-First Design

The smart contract prevents minting unless the vault balance (verified by oracle) exceeds the current token supply. No paper promises.

Batch Settlement

User orders are aggregated; metal is acquired and vaulted in bulk to optimize premiums and logistics before tokens are distributed.

Segregated Compliance

A dual-layer access control system separates users based on jurisdiction (India vs. Global) to manage redemption rights legally.

4. System Architecture

01. Off-Chain Infrastructure

  • Custodians: Insured, third-party vaults holding allocated London Good Delivery silver.
  • Auditors: Independent entities performing physical inventory reconciliation.
  • Settlement Oracles: Data feeds bridging off-chain vault reports to on-chain contracts.

02. On-Chain Contracts

  • DST_Contract: ERC-20 compliant token representing ownership. Pausable and burnable.
  • MintingController: Privileged contract. Only mints upon receiving a signed message from the Auditor/Oracle confirming a new deposit.
  • JurisdictionRegistry: Whitelists wallets based on KYC status and region.

Settlement & Minting Flow

The protocol rejects real-time minting for a secure, audited lifecycle:

01

Aggregation

T_cutoff

Users deposit fiat; orders are batched until a specific cutoff time is reached.

02

Acquisition & Vaulting

Treasury purchases physical silver; metal is delivered to the secure Vault.

03

Verification

Vault issues a Warehouse Receipt. Auditor signs a cryptographic proof of deposit.

04

Minting

Oracle relays proof to the MintingController. DST_Contract mints tokens exactly matching the new silver weight (1 DST = 1g).

05

Distribution

Tokens are airdropped to user wallets.
